Question_CN: 这张氢原子能级图说明了什么？（参考幻灯片 20-21）
Question_EN: What does this energy-level diagram of a hydrogen atom illustrate? (Refer to slides 20-21)
Answer_CN: 这是一张氢原子（或类氢原子）的能级图。1. 电子能量（E_n）（纵轴）： 显示电子在不同状态下的能量，单位为电子伏特（eV）。负值表示电子被“束缚”在原子核上。2. n（主量子数）： 标记能级。n=1 是最低（基态）；n=2,3...是较高（激发态）；n=∞ 意味着电子是自由的（电离的）。3. 基态（n=1）： 最低能级（-13.6 eV）。这里的电子被束缚得最紧。4. 激发态（n=2,3,4,5）： 更高的能级（负值较小）。这里的电子具有更多能量但仍被束缚。5. 电离能（E_i）： 将电子从基态（n=1）释放到自由状态（n=∞）所需的能量。对于氢，是 13.6 eV（基态能量的绝对值）。6. 能量连续区（n=∞）： 当 E=0（或正值）时，电子是自由的（不束缚于原子核），可以具有任何动能（E=KE）。
Answer_EN: This is an energy-level diagram of a hydrogen atom (or a hydrogen-like atom). 1. Electron energy (E_n) (vertical axis): Shows the energy of an electron in different states, in electron volts (eV). Negative values mean the electron is "bound" to the nucleus. 2. n (principal quantum number): Labels energy levels. n=1 is the lowest (ground state); n=2,3... are higher (excited states); n=∞ means the electron is free (ionized). 3. Ground state (n=1): The lowest energy level (−13.6 eV). Electrons here are most tightly bound. 4. Excited states (n=2,3,4,5): Higher energy levels (less negative). Electrons here have more energy but are still bound. 5. Ionization energy (E_i): The energy needed to free the electron from the ground state (n=1 to n=∞). For hydrogen, it's 13.6 eV (the absolute value of the ground-state energy). 6. Continuum of energy (n=∞): When E=0 (or positive), the electron is free (not bound to the nucleus) and can have any kinetic energy (E=KE).

Question_CN: 这张图对比了哪三种半导体？（参考幻灯片 126,128）

Question_EN: Explain why electrons and holes move in opposite directions when an electric field is applied to a semiconductor. (Refer to slides 48-50)
Answer_CN: 电子带负电，受电场力作用与电场方向相反；空穴等效带正电，受电场力作用与电场方向相同。因此，它们在电场中朝相反方向漂移。
Answer_EN: Electrons are negatively charged and experience a force opposite to the electric field direction; holes behave as positive charges and experience a force in the same direction as the electric field. Consequently, they drift in opposite directions.
